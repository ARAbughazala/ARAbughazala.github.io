Jar Sorter

Automation Design Project, December 13, 2016

Objective

Create an automated machine that can identify faults in the process of applying the lids to peanut butter jars

Description

This project was one of the first projects i worked on as a Mechatronic Engineering Student. Our professor acted as a client that was part of a peanut butter plant. He was having trouble with some of machines not applying the lids on correctly, and had no way of knowing whether the jars are correctly being packaged or not, losing efficiency because the plant would check the lids at the end of the process. Using B&R's PP35 Power Panel Controller, switches, pneumatic actuators and ladder logic I worked with a team of 2 mechanical engineering Students and we came up with a way to check whether the lids were on, off, or crossthreaded after lids are applied, than separating the bad jars from the good ones, while not stopping nor slowing down the line.


LEGO Pole Climber Bot

Machine Design Final Project, May 15, 2018

Objective

Create a pole climbing bot that can pick up a block, and pass through the time trap

Description

This was a machine design project created by my professor Dr.Chuen Hsu to test our understanding of key machine design ideas. My team and i first started by creating a pugh chart to choose which of our designs we want to implement, seeing how the provided power supply was from LEGO manufacturers it gave Alex's LEGO design the lead. We than created multiple iterations of better bots as we completed testing. Finally we created a lego bot that is compact, had a great center of mass and weight distribution relative to the pole. My team who is Group #1 on the testing list, ended up having the highest efficiency in a bot in our entire class.


UART Microcontroller Game

Digital System Design Final Project, May 10, 2018

Objective

Create serial communication between 2 microcontrollers

Description

My team and i decided that we wanted to create a game to implement UART serial communication as it would be a fun way of doing so. We first created a schematic to guide us on how we are wiring the controllers as well as initializing ports to be used. The game works by the players pressing the start button(reset on board) and they must wait for a tune to be over, once it is they have to react quickly and start pressing their button to reach 100 first, the winner ends the communication or game and the imperial match from star wars is played for him.
